# Research: Contra Series Per-Entry Differentiation

## Overview

Each Contra entry differentiates itself through **one or two added systems** layered on the 1987 base loop (run / jump / 8-way shoot / weapon swap / 1-hit death). Identifying these layered systems gives Echo a reuse menu — adopt the systems that survived the series' verdict, reject the ones that failed (Source: [[Contra Per Entry Mechanic Matrix]]).

## Key Findings

- **Contra III: The Alien Wars (1992, SNES)**: introduced **two-weapon dual-wield carry** (one per arm, fire-button-per-shoulder) and **Mode 7 rotational top-down stages**. Two-weapon carry survived into Contra 4, Hard Corps, Operation Galuga; Mode 7 perspective stages were dropped after this entry (Source: [[Contra III The Alien Wars]], https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contra_III:_The_Alien_Wars).
- **Contra: Hard Corps (1994, Genesis)**: introduced **branching paths (4 routes / 5 endings)**, **character select with unique movesets** (sliding i-frame, Browny hover), and **vehicle stages (Motoroid hoverbike)**. Branching narrative is the single most-cited innovation in Hard Corps and was revived in Hard Corps: Uprising (Source: [[Contra Hard Corps]], https://contra.fandom.com/wiki/Contra:_Hard_Corps).
- **Contra: Shattered Soldier (2002, PS2)**: introduced the **Hit Rate grading system** (0–100% per stage, D→S rank, gates true ending). The first Contra to make scoring an explicit progression gate rather than bragging rights (Source: [[Hit Rate Grading System]], https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contra:_Shattered_Soldier).
- **Neo Contra (2004, PS2)**: introduced **isometric/over-the-shoulder perspective** and a **3-slot weapon set** (standard / power / lock-on). L1 instant-swap + L2 lock-trajectory + R2 free-rotate. Hit Rate carried over from Shattered Soldier (Source: [[Neo Contra]], https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neo_Contra).
- **Contra 4 (2007, DS)**: introduced **dual-screen vertical playfield with grappling hook** (latch onto railings to traverse screens) and rebooted the inventory to **two-weapon carry from Contra III**, dropping Hit Rate (Source: [[Wikipedia Contra Series]], https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contra_4).
- **Hard Corps: Uprising (2011, XBLA/PSN)**: introduced **Rising Mode roguelite-lite metaprogression** (Score → CP shop → permanent HP/weapon-speed/maneuver upgrades) alongside an **Arcade Mode** that strips them. First explicit progression-vs-purist split in the series (Source: [[Hard Corps Uprising]], https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hard_Corps:_Uprising).
- **Contra: Rogue Corps (2019, multi)**: introduced **angled top-down twin-stick** + **character classes** (Kaiser, The Gentleman, Ms. Harakiri, Hungry Beast) + **weapon overheating cooldown meter** (press-to-fire, not hold). Critically panned — the third 3D-perspective failure in the series (Source: [[Contra Rogue Corps]], https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contra:_Rogue_Corps).
- **Contra: Operation Galuga (2024, multi)**: introduced **Health Bar (multi-hit per life)**, **Dash + Double Jump**, **2-weapon overload swap**, and **Perk Shop** (in-run credits buy 2 equippable perks: extra HP, longer i-frames, start-with-laser, etc.). Returned to 2D after Rogue Corps (Source: [[Contra Operation Galuga]], https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Contra:_Operation_Galuga, https://www.pcgamer.com/games/action/the-new-contra-operation-galuga-crushed-my-skepticism-with-new-ideas-that-actually-work/).

## Per-Entry Differentiation Pattern (Verdict)

> [!key-insight] Survival rule
> Systems that **stayed in 2D and added one new mechanical axis** survived to later entries (two-weapon carry, branching paths, sliding, perk shop). Systems that **changed the perspective** (3D or top-down) were rejected — 1996 Legacy of War, 1998 Contra Adventure, 2019 Rogue Corps all underperformed and were followed by 2D returns.

## Contradictions

- **Hit Rate intent**. Wikipedia frames it as a difficulty gate; fan reviews ([[Wikipedia Contra Series]]) frame it as a replay incentive. Both true: it gates true ending **and** drives replay — the ambiguity is consistent across sources.
- **Dual-wield duration**. Some sources call Contra III's two-weapon carry "carried throughout series"; in fact it disappeared in Shattered Soldier and Neo Contra and only returned in Contra 4 (and revised as 2-overload-swap in Galuga). Treat as **partial survival, not continuous**.

## Open Questions

- Why did **Mode 7 rotational stages** (Contra III) never return despite being praised? Hypothesis: Genesis/PS1 successors lacked the SNES-specific gimmick, then PS2/PS3 era prioritized 3D over flat rotation. Verify with Konami postmortems if findable.
- Does **Operation Galuga's Perk Shop** function as the *production-canonical* successor to Hard Corps: Uprising's Rising Mode, or is it a separate lineage from arcade-style "credits unlock weapons"? Frame as design-lineage open question.
- Echo applicability: which **one** of these systems is the strongest fit alongside time-rewind? (See [[Solo Contra 2026 Concept]]: time-rewind already locked as differentiator; second axis must not stack cognitive load.)
