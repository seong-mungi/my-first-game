# Wikipedia: Metal Slug (series)

## Source Type
Encyclopedia reference covering the full Metal Slug series, mechanics, and commercial history.

## Key Claims

### Origins
- Developed by Nazca Corporation (later merged into SNK), published by SNK.
- Released April 19, 1996 on Neo Geo MVS arcade; Neo Geo AES home console May 24, 1996.
- Core concept: "simple yet exciting side-scrolling shooter with an easy control scheme."
- Visual inspiration: Hayao Miyazaki. Gameplay lineage: Irem's In the Hunt and GunForce II.
- Original design was slower-paced and tank-focused; redesigned after poor location test response.

### Mechanics
- Player starts with semi-automatic handgun (unlimited ammo).
- Weapon pickups replace current weapon; ammo depletes → reverts to handgun.
- Metal Slug 6+: carry and switch between 3 weapons simultaneously.
- **Melee**: Contact with enemies triggers melee opportunity; melee is stronger than most shots.
- **Vehicle (Slug)**: SV-001 has vulcan (unlimited) + cannon (limited ammo). Survives 3 hits. Exit: Down + Jump. Flashes red when near destruction.
- **POW rescue**: Rewards items and score. POW count resets to zero on player death — creates risk/reward tension.
- Six missions per entry (most titles); MS3/5/6: 5 missions; MS7: 7 missions.

### Series Character Roster
- Original: Marco Rossi, Tarma Roving.
- Later added: Eri Kasamoto, Fiolina "Fio" Germi → "quintessential Metal Slug team" of four.

### Franchise Expansion
- Spin-offs include: third-person shooter, tower defense (Metal Slug Defense), turn-based tactics (Metal Slug Tactics, 2024).
- SNK celebrated 30 years in April 2026 with tease of future projects.
