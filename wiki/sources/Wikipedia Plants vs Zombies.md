# Wikipedia: Plants vs. Zombies (video game)

## Summary

The Wikipedia article is the most accessible canonical reference for Plants vs. Zombies' design history, mechanics, and reception. Treat it as a starting point: it's well-cited, but for deep design analysis, follow the citations to original interviews and design talks.

## What It Contributes

- **Genre origin claim.** Establishes PvZ as the canonical lane defense reference.
- **Designer rationale.** Documents George Fan's stated reasoning for the lane design.
- **Hard numbers.** Plant counts, zombie counts, environment categories — useful as reference baselines.
- **Mode taxonomy.** Adventure, Mini-games, Puzzle, Survival, Zen Garden, I-Zombie — useful for thinking about content extension patterns.

## What's Missing

- **Tuning data.** Plant costs, sun rates, HP values — not in the article body. Have to find via gameplay wikis.
- **Designer interviews.** Mostly second-hand summaries; primary sources (GDC talks, podcasts) carry more design weight.
- **Sequel comparison.** PvZ 2's F2P design choices are documented separately.

## Notes on Confidence

Wikipedia summaries are generally accurate for high-traffic articles like this one. Specific numbers (49 plants, 26 zombies) are verifiable against the official PopCap materials. Designer-rationale quotes are at one remove from the source — high confidence on direction, medium on exact wording.
