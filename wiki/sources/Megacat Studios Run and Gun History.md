# Mega Cat Studios: A History of Run-and-Gun Shooters

## Source Type
Game culture article from indie developer Mega Cat Studios. Practical historical overview with era breakdown.

## Key Claims By Era

### Origins (1975–1979)
- Gun Fight (1975): early shooting game ancestry.
- Sheriff (1979): closer ancestor — separated shooting direction from movement.

### Golden Age (1980s)
- Robotron: 2084 (1982): top-down twin-stick "mow down enemies while maintaining mobility" template.
- **Contra (1987)**: transformative. Combined "platforming with eight-directional shooting." NES port influential via superior sound and visual optimization.

### 1990s Evolution
- Metal Slug series: inherited Contra's DNA, added "cartoonishly violent" gameplay with "lavish art and animation."
- Also: Gunstar Heroes, Zombies Ate My Neighbours, Super Star Wars series.

### Modern Revival (2000s–Present)
- Alien Hominid: browser → console bridge.
- **Cuphead (2017)** and **Blazing Chrome (2019)**: demonstrate "enduring appeal of high-speed shooting gameplay paired with demanding difficulty curves."
