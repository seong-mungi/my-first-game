# Contra III: The Alien Wars (1992)

SNES run-and-gun by Konami. Defining innovations: **dual-weapon carry (one per arm)** and **Mode 7 top-down rotational stages**.

## Differentiated Systems

| System | Detail | Survived To |
|---|---|---|
| Two-weapon carry | One weapon per arm, fire-button per shoulder, instant swap | Contra 4, Operation Galuga (as overload-swap) |
| Mode 7 top-down | 2 of 6 stages played overhead with player-fixed rotating world via L/R buttons | NOT carried forward — single-entry experiment |
| Sprite-scaling set pieces | 16-bit hardware-leveraged spectacle (giant fingers, pursuing missiles) | Boilerplate post-Genesis |

## Notes

The Mode 7 control scheme — character always faces up, world rotates — was praised by reviewers ("well integrated, not a gimmick") but disappeared from the series after this entry. PS-era successors moved to 3D rather than continuing flat rotation.
