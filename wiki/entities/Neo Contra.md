# Neo Contra (2004)

PS2 by Team Neo Kijirushi. Perspective experiment: **isometric / over-the-shoulder**, with mostly-isometric stages plus occasional side-scrolling and overhead-behind sections. Hit Rate carried over from [[Contra Shattered Soldier]].

## Differentiated Systems

| System | Detail | Survived To |
|---|---|---|
| Isometric / OTS | Camera elevated 45° behind player; aim by character facing | NOT carried forward — single-entry perspective |
| 3-slot weapon set | Standard / Power / Lock-on weapons per set; 3 sets initial + 4 unlock | (Influenced multi-weapon eras) |
| L1 instant swap | Toggle between standard ↔ power on shoulder button | Operation Galuga 2-weapon swap (modernized) |
| L2 lock-trajectory | Hold L2 to fire-direction-lock while moving | Twin-stick standard since |
| R2 free-rotate | Hold R2 to rotate in place without moving | Twin-stick standard since |
| Lock-on for air targets | Triangle-hold paints anti-air targets | NOT in 2D successors |

## Reception

Mixed — innovative inputs but narrow appeal post-Shattered Soldier. Marked the second 3D-perspective deviation in the series; though arguably *succeeded* as an isolated experiment more than Legacy of War (1996) did.
